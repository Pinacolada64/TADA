c:messages. Message #14

Shields in Monster Combat

There are two phases: First it is determined whether or not you deflected part
of the attack with your shield, or the monster managed to slip past it. If you
did deflect part of the attack, the second phase is to determine how much
damage was absorbed by the shield.

Phase 1; did the shield take hits.
Base probability = 20 + (player level times 10). Maximum base of 80%
Formal shield training: +20%
Small monster: -10%
Swift monster: -20%
Large monster: +10%
Huge monster:  +20%

Phase 2; assuming the shield takes damage, how much.
Base damage absorbed = 3 + (% shield/25)
  ie: for every 25% of shield, 1 additional damage point is absorbed.

Formal shield training: +1 damage
Paladin: +2 damage

There is a chance that larger monsters may rip the shield out of your hands.
Probability: Huge=16%, Large=13%, Big=10%, Man sized=6%
If Formal shield training: probability -3%

Notice that your shield size affects its ability to absorb damage. Also not
that this is reduced every time it does take damage.

   how many guild  members are  present when  you  are  attacked. This  applies
   regardless of who is  attacking  and who is defending.

5) If a fellow guild member  is present while you are lying unconscious  (having
   lost a duel), he  will  protect  you from being LOOTed during your  absence.

7) You will have access to your guild's private sub.

[]=-=-=-=-=[ SPUR DUEL HELP ]=-=-=-=-=[]

You will have two operating menus, the main one is active when you are standing
up. The secondary one is active when you are lying on the ground.

The main menu is:

(A] P,B,F,R,H,?)

A: Is an all out attack against your opponent. It can inflict more hits, but
leaves you more vulnerable to shield bashes and Attacks by your opponent.

P: Parry with your weapon. This represents a dodging, darting sort of tactic.
It will be harder for your opponent to hit you, and if he does, he will not do
quite as much damage. If you should Parry the same turn that your opponent
tries to Shield bash, there is a chance you will catch him off guard and trip
him. Making HIM the one to fall down.
   You may still score a hit on your opponent while Parrying, though at
somewhat reduced effectiveness.

B: Shield bash and attempt to knock your opponent down. If you knock him down,
you will strike at much enhanced effectiveness in the turn in which he falls,
and also in the next turn when he tries to stand back up!
   To see how various factors affect the shield bash, turn your 'V' on to
see a  running commentary.

F: Flee from Duel (You cannot use this if dueling to the death!)

R: Ready a weapon. You must have a weapon readied unless fighting with fists.
---------------------------------------
If you are knocked down, your secondary menu will be activated. It contains:
"S]tand up, R)oll, H)"  Standing up, attempts to stand back up. R)oll, rolls
on the ground. This is a combination defensive, offensive maneuver. At worst,
it reduces somewhat, the damage you will take. At best, your opponent will trip
over you if he either attacks or bashes (but not parries). If he trips over
you, you are both on the ground, and must stand up. There is also a chance you
may strike from the ground when rolling, though at reduced effectiveness. 'H'
gets this help message.
----------------------------------------
Note that rooms with '+' following their title are FREE FIRE zones. That means
that anybody may challenge anybody else as long as they are conscious. It also
means that Autoduel is not effective in those squares.
----------------------------------------
Sizes:
  10-Humans, Ogres, Half-elves
   9-Elfs, Dwarves, Orcs
   8-Hobbits, Gnomes
   7-Pixies
-----------------------------------------
Tips:
   > Keep your energy and dexterity up (especially when leaving the game!). If  
     you are tired or clumsy, you will probably spend a lot of time on the ground.
> Keep in mind that the smaller races have an advantage when maneuvering
during a shield bash (smaller size makes them quicker).
> If you have the Ring, USE it before leaving the game (or when dueling
somebody else). This will make you harder to see, and there is a chance they
will 'lose sight of you' when they swing.

c:messages. Message #32
The EXAMINE command in Spur.

The EXAMINE command is the act of looking at an object closely. You may specify
the object to be examined by typing the name after it (ie: EXAMINE ROCKS) or
simply enter EXAMINE to examine everything in the room. NOTE: simply entering
X is the same as entering EXAMINE.
  As some objects lying about are cursed, booby-trapped, or otherwise unhealthy
to pick up, it is a good idea to examine every object you want to pick up,
until you get an idea of what is safe, and what is not.
  Successfully examining an object that is not healthy for you, will raise your
intelligence (up to 25). This is a good tactic to use to get smarter, even if
you already know an object is bad from a previous examination.
  Often, your attempt at examining an object will fail. Just keep re-examining
it and you will succeed sooner or later.
  Examine dead monsters! Sometimes, you will discover hidden treasure on them,
and sometimes you will decide that they are (ugh) edible. Dwarves are more
likely to find hidden treasure, while Orcs and Ogres are more likely to decide
that they are edible (Elves and half-elves are less likely to eat dead
monsters). Note that there is a small chance that you will pick up a disease
from examining a dead monster! (Apple a Day spell and the Blue Pill cures
disease).
  Examining another player in your room, will reveal much information about
that character.
  While on The Space Ship it is a GOOD idea to examine EVERY room, even if
there doesn't appear to be anything there. This is also true to some extent in
the Land of Oz.

c:messages. Message #41
The Track Command:

This command allows you to attempt to track down other players in the dungeon.

You have a base track range (in squares) of 12. This is modified as follows:

You are a Wizard           : -2
You are a Ranger           : +5
You are a Thief            : +2
You are an Assassin        : +2
You have a TRACKER ally    : +5
Your player level          : +1 for each level.

Target is an Elf           : -2
Target is a Hobbit         : -3
Target is a Gnome          : -3
Target is a Pixie          : -5
Target is a fighter        : +2
Target is a Knight         : +2
Targets player level       : -1 for each level.

Range is calculated to the target as the sum of the squares in two directions.
Ie: if he were 3 squares east, and 6 squares south, the range would be 9.
If the range to the target is equal or less than, your tracking range as
determined about, you are considered to have detected the target. A general
range and direction will be given. (ie: YOU DETECT JOE-BLOW TO THE NORTHEAST AT
A CONSIDERABLE DISTANCE).

You should check this file periodically for any changes to the routine.
